Destinations

Ohiya

Ohiya is a serene mountain village in Sri Lanka’s Central Highlands, perched at around 1,774 meters above sea level. It’s best known as the gateway to Horton Plains National Park, offering access to dramatic viewpoints like World’s End and lush trekking trails such as the Devil’s Staircase1. With misty landscapes, scenic train rides, and nearby waterfalls like Bambarakanda Falls, Ohiya is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers.


Haputale

Haputale is a charming mountain town in Sri Lanka’s Uva Province, surrounded by lush tea plantations and misty cloud forests. It offers breathtaking views from spots like Lipton’s Seat and is home to colonial-era landmarks such as the Adisham Bungalow1.

Galle

Galle is a historic coastal city in southern Sri Lanka, famed for its 16th-century Galle Fort—a UNESCO World Heritage Site blending colonial architecture with vibrant local culture2. With scenic beaches, the iconic lighthouse, and charming streets filled with cafés and artisan shops, Galle offers a captivating mix of heritage and seaside charm2.
